Last Sunday, my family and I went to the city zoo. I was very exciting because I haven't been there for a long time. We arrived to the zoo at nine o'clock in the morning, just when it opened. The first animal we saw was a giant panda. It spent the whole morning to eat bamboo leaves. I couldn't help to laugh when I saw it rolling around on the grass. The zookeeper told us that pandas spends about twelve hours a day eating. My little sister said she want to be a panda when she grows up. Next, we visited the penguin area. The reason I love penguins is because they looks so funny when they walk. There was several baby penguins standing near the water. They were afraid from jumping into the cold pool. Suddenly, one brave penguin jumped in, and the others followed it. I took many photos, but most of them were too dark. After lunch, we went to see the snake house. I am afraid of snakes, so I waited outside while my dad and my brother went in. My brother said the snakes was amazing, but I didn't believe him. Then we walked to the bird section. I saw a colourful parrot that can say "hello" and "goodbye." It reminds me about a pet parrot my grandmother used to have. At the end of the day, we watched the dolphin show. The dolphins jumped so high, it was incredible. The trainer said that dolphins are one of the smartest animal in the world. I was very tiring after walking around all day, but I felt happy. My mum told us that we should be proud for taking care of the animals and the environment. This trip taught me something important. Although zoos are fun, but we must also remember to protect animals in the wild. Every time I look at the photos I took that day, I feel grateful for such a wonderful experience. I hope I can go back there again soon.

I will never forget the day our school held its annual Science Fair. It happened two months ago at the school hall. My best friend Lucas and I decided to join it together. We spent two weeks to prepare our project, which was about how plants grow under different colours of light. At first, everything went smooth. We bought three small plants and put them in different boxes. One box had red light, another had blue light, and the last one had no special light. We kept to check the plants every morning before class. I found my brave to ask our science teacher, Mr. Chen, for some advice. He was very helpful and even lend us a book about photosynthesis. On the day of the fair, I felt very nervous. There is hundreds of students and teachers walking around the hall. Everyone in our class were excited but also a little scared. When the judges arrived to our table, my heart started pounding. They asked us many questions, but we answered them confident. Lucas explained how we designed the experiment, and I showed them our results. The judges looked very interested on our project. One of them said that our idea was very creative. However, they also pointed out a mistake we had made. We forgot to record the temperature in each box, so our data was not complete. I felt a little disappointed, but I knew they were right. After the fair, we didn't win first prize, but we still felt proud for what we had done. Although we made a mistake, but we learned a valuable lesson. We learned that being careful and accurate is more important than just having a good idea. The reason our project didn't win is because we were too careless with the details. Now, every time I do an experiment, I always remind myself to record everything carefully. I also learned that failing is not a bad thing — it is a chance to become better. Next year, Lucas and I are already planning our new project. I am sure we will do better, because this experience taught us a lot. I am really looking forward to participate in the next Science Fair.
